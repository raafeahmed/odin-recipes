Keema Samosa

Description

Keema Samosa is a popular Pakistani snack with a filling of keema enclosed in a flour based pastry and deep fried. Enjoy it hot with mint chutney on the side.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams chicken mince / qeema
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • few drops of oil
  • 1 teaspoon roasted cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/4 teaspoon red chili flakes
  • 3 - 4 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 cups coriander, finely chopped
  • 2 green chili, sliced
  • 40 - 50 samosa patti or spring roll wrappers, cut into required size
  • 4 - 5 tablespoon flour
  • Oil for frying

Steps

  1. Put the mince and water (about 1 1/2 cups - 2 cups) in a saucepan.
  2. Add salt, red chili powder, ginger paste, garlic paste, coriander powder, and a few drops of oil.
  3. Bring the mince to a boil, and cook until water is evaporated and mince is cooked through.
  4. Take out the mince in a large bowl, and set it aside to cool.
  5. Add roasted cumin seeds, garam masala, and red chili flakes o the cooked mince and mix well. At this point, the mince mixture can be kept in the freezer, if needed.
  6. Add the onion, coriander, and green chili and mix through.
  7. Mix flour and water in a small bowl to make a thick paste.
  8. Fill the samosa patti with the mince mixture(about 1 - 2 teaspoon of the filling), and fold it into a triangular shape using the sticky flour mixture to glue it shut.
  9. The samosa can be fried off right away, or frozen to use another day.
  10. To freeze, place the samosas on a tray making sure they don't touch each other. Keep the tray in the freezer for about 30 - 45 minutes till the samosas are frozen. Once the samosas are frozen, they can be removed individually and put in a plastic bag. Spray oil on the tray to make sure the samosas don't stick to the bottom.
  11. To fry, just take the frozen samosas out of the freezer and place in medium heat oil. Fry till golden brown. Frozen samosas will take 1 - 2 minutes longer to cook compared to fresh samosas.
